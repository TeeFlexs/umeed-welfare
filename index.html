About Us

Our Story of Hope

Umeed Welfare Group was born from a simple yet profound belief — that access to healthcare is a human right, not a privilege.

Our Journey

From a Single Camp
to a Movement

2019
The First Camp
Syed Basim Qamar Jaffery organized the very first free medical camp in a small village in Punjab, serving 40 patients with a team of 5 volunteers.
2020
Partnership with VFAHT
Formal partnership established with VFAHT, bringing specialized medical professionals and expanded diagnostic capabilities to our camps.
2021
Jilani Welfare Joins
The Jilani Welfare Association became a key collaborator, helping extend our reach to KPK province and underserved border communities.
2022
Scaling Up Operations
Introduced mobile medical units, allowing us to reach remote areas inaccessible by traditional means. 500 patients served in a single camp.
2024
2,400+ Lives Touched
Today, Umeed Welfare Group operates across 6 districts with 120+ volunteers, a permanent pharmacy supply chain, and ongoing community health programs.
S
Syed Basim Qamar Jaffery
Founder & Director

"I remember the day a mother carried her child for three hours just to reach our camp. That moment confirmed everything. Healthcare is not a privilege — it is a right. As long as I breathe, Umeed Welfare Group will fight for that right for every person, regardless of their means."

Mission, Vision & Values

Our Mission

To provide free, dignified, and quality healthcare services to underprivileged communities across Pakistan through organized medical camps, medicine distribution, and preventive health education.

Our Vision

A Pakistan where every citizen, regardless of income or location, has access to essential healthcare services. A society where no one suffers preventable disease due to lack of resources or access.

Our Values

  • Compassion without condition
  • Full transparency & accountability
  • Dignity for every patient
  • Community-driven solutions
  • Islamic ethics & humanitarian law

Our Programs

Healthcare Programs
That Transform Lives

Every program is designed to address a specific barrier to healthcare access faced by underprivileged communities.

Free Medical Camps
Our flagship program organizes large-scale free medical camps in rural and urban slum areas. Each camp features qualified doctors across multiple specialties, nurses, diagnostic equipment, and a complete on-site pharmacy. Patients receive full consultations, diagnostics, and medicines — all free of cost, with complete dignity.
Program Highlights:
  • -- Multi-specialty consultations (General, Gynecology, Pediatrics, ENT)
  • -- Full diagnostic suite on-site
  • -- Free medicines dispensed at each camp
  • -- Women-only consultation areas for privacy

Healthcare Supplies & Medicines
We maintain a dedicated pharmacy supply chain, sourcing quality medicines at low cost and distributing them free to patients. Our supply program also provides essential healthcare supplies — bandages, syringes, diagnostic equipment — to areas with no pharmacy access. We partner with pharmaceutical companies for bulk procurement.
Impact So Far:
  • -- 5,200+ individual medicines distributed
  • -- Chronic disease management medications
  • -- Paediatric and maternal health supplies
  • -- Emergency medical kits for remote areas

Basic Diagnostic Services
Early detection saves lives. Our diagnostic program brings portable testing equipment to underserved communities — blood glucose monitoring, blood pressure, hemoglobin testing, urine analysis, and basic ECG services. Trained paramedics and nurses administer tests with laboratory-grade accuracy and immediate results.

Community Outreach & Education
Prevention is better than cure. Our outreach program educates communities about basic hygiene, nutrition, maternal health, childhood vaccination schedules, and chronic disease management. We work with local religious leaders, schools, and community councils to create sustainable health awareness that lasts long after our camps leave.
Key Initiatives:
  • -- Maternal & Child Health Workshops
  • -- School Health Programs for 500+ students
  • -- Hygiene & Sanitation Awareness
  • -- Local health ambassador training
